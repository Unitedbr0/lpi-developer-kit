## Summary

Artificial Intelligence is transforming industries but also consumes high energy and computational resources. This raises sustainability concerns.

The SMILE framework helps make AI systems efficient and eco-friendly using simulation, digital twins, and continuous optimisation.

### Phase 1: Reality Emulation  
Create a digital twin of the system to simulate behaviour and energy usage.

### Phase 2: Concurrent Engineering  
Test different models and strategies in a simulated environment to find the most efficient solution.

### Phase 3: Collective Intelligence  
Collect real-time data from systems like servers and GPUs for monitoring.

### Phase 4: Contextual Intelligence  
Optimize performance dynamically based on system conditions.

### Phase 5: Continuous Intelligence  
Suggest improvements like model compression and efficient retraining.

### Phase 6: Perpetual Wisdom  
Share knowledge globally to improve sustainable AI practices.

## Conclusion

SMILE provides a structured way to build energy-efficient AI systems. It reduces computational waste, improves decision-making, and promotes environmentally responsible AI development.
